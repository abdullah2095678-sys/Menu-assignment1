Our Story

The Spirit of the Izakaya

居酒屋の精神
炎
Since 2019

Where Ember Meets
Winter Night

Kuroyuki means "black snow" in Japanese. The restaurant started in 2019 with one goal: to bring the feel of a real Tokyo izakaya to Toronto. The robata grill uses white-oak charcoal sourced from Wakayama Prefecture, and every dish is made with care — keeping things simple and honest, the way Japanese cooking is meant to be.

🌿 Vegetarian Friendly 🌊 Fresh Daily Fish 🍶 75+ Sakes

Our chefs train in the omakase style — fully trusting the guest's experience. We source Wagyu from Miyazaki, salmon from Hokkaido, and tofu made fresh in the kitchen every morning.

The vibe is calm and cozy — low lighting, cedar wood, quiet jazz, and the sound of skewers grilling over coals. It's the kind of place where you want to stay for a while.
